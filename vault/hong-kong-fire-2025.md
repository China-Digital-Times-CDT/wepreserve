A catastrophic fire at Wang Fuk Court in Tai Po, Hong Kong, engulfed seven of eight residential towers on November 26, 2025—the city's deadliest fire since 1948. Authorities cite gross negligence in renovation works as a key driver of the blaze and its rapid spread.

Status snapshot (2026-03-19): 168 dead (58 males, 110 females; youngest 6 months, oldest 98; all identified via forensic analysis); 79 injured; one firefighter confirmed dead; 10 foreign domestic workers dead and 5 injured. Government announced HK$6.8b buyback plan (Feb 21) at HK$8,000–10,500/sq ft; redevelopment ruled out. First evidential hearing held Mar 19: lead lawyer says most likely cause was cigarettes igniting materials in an air shaft; "nearly all fire safety systems failed because of human factors." Police arrested 38; ICAC arrested 23. Over 300 residents petitioning for a homeowners' meeting.

## Key Facts (as of 2026-03-19)
- **Impact and scale**: Fire began ~2:52 p.m. on Nov 26, 2025 at Wang Fuk Court, Tai Po (~2,000 flats/~4,600 residents; ~40% aged 65+). Seven of eight towers burned; 200 fire trucks, 100 ambulances, and >800 firefighters/paramedics deployed. Building fatalities: Wang Tai 82, Wang Cheong 70, Wang Sun 3, Wang Do 2, Wang Shing 1; none in Wang Yan/Wang Kin/Wang Chi; one victim not yet tied to a block (UDN/Ming Pao/TVBS/DotDotNews). SPCA estimates >537 animals affected; 364 found (294 survived, 70 died, 173 missing) ([RTHK](https://news.rthk.hk/rthk/ch/component/k2/1834467-20251203.htm)).
- **Materials and systems**: Highly flammable polystyrene and foam panels blocked windows and escape routes; alarms found non-functional across all towers (some residents say alarms were switched off during works). Officials say 7 of 20 scaffold-net samples failed fire-resistance tests; allegations of mesh swapping to evade inspections ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/hong-wang-china-dissent-fire-construction-ff953aec2bc0201b0e3255805a241fc1)). Elevator-lobby windows reportedly sealed with foam boards; residents complained in 2024 about flammable netting/foam ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/11/28/world/asia/hong-kong-fire-officials-warned.html)). PolyU’s Xinyan Huang later clarified remarks on bamboo’s role and urged caution pending a full technical study.
- **Oversight and investigations**: Police have arrested 38 people (manslaughter, fraud); 9 charged. ICAC arrested 23 (bribery, conspiracy to defraud). ICAC probe made multiple arrests linked to the renovation project, including on Dec 17 the current OC chairperson Tsui Mun-kam (徐滿柑) and former chairperson Tang Kwok-kuen (鄧國權), who signed the HK$330m contract during his five consecutive terms as chair (2012–Sep 2024) ([on.cc](https://hk.on.cc/hk/bkn/cnt/news/20251217/bkn-20251217175140460-1217_00822_001.html)). Chief Executive John Lee announced a non-statutory judge-led committee, promising administrative directives in lieu of a statutory inquiry; on Dec 12 he said the panel chaired by Judge David Lok will report within nine months and will probe systemic construction risks, conflicts, collusion, and bid-rigging ([Tweet](https://x.com/haileyhmt/status/1995860431177449729?s=46) [Reuters/Japan Times](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2025/12/12/asia-pacific/hong-kong-fire-probe/)). The committee held its first public meeting on Feb 5; Senior Counsel Victor Dawes said the evidence shows "a series of systemic problems that should not be neglected and are unacceptable." On Mar 17, the committee published a list of 37 involved parties for 8 evidential hearings (Mar 19–Apr 2), including 14 former OC members (notably arrested chairman Tsui Moon-come), 7 residents, directors of contractor Prestige Construction and consultant Will Power Architects, representatives of 9 government departments/statutory bodies (Buildings Dept, FSD, police, ICAC, URA), ISS East Point (property manager), Victory Fire Engineering, and Hop On Management ([HKFP](https://hongkongfp.com/2026/02/05/residents-of-fire-hit-estate-urge-accountability-compensation-as-review-committee-holds-first-public-meeting/) [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/society/article/3346891/21-wang-fuk-court-homeowners-residents-give-evidence-fire-probe)). Mandatory Building Inspection Scheme tender reportedly jumped from ~HK$140m to HK$330m; residents alleged bid-rigging and triad-linked interference. URA says its 100/100 “project management” score for the main contractor under the Building Rehab Company Registration Scheme will be clarified and the scoring method revised ([HKEJ](https://www.hkej.com/instantnews/article?id=4264192)).
- **Response and support**: Insurance Authority task force coordinating outreach; ~8,700 policies tied to Wang Fuk Court (1,100 general, 7,600 life) identified ([Yahoo](https://hk.news.yahoo.com/%E5%AE%8F%E7%A6%8F%E8%8B%91%E4%BA%94%E7%B4%9A%E7%81%AB%EF%BD%9C%E4%BF%9D%E7%9B%A3%E5%B1%80%EF%BC%9A%E6%B6%89%E5%AE%8F%E7%A6%8F%E8%8B%91%E4%BF%9D%E5%96%AE%E5%85%B1-8700-%E5%BC%B5-%E5%B7%B2%E8%A8%AD%E5%B0%88%E8%B2%AC%E5%B0%8F%E7%B5%84%E5%8D%94%E8%AA%BF%EF%BD%9Cyahoo-073800120.html)). Donations tracker "Taipo Big Donations Watcher" lists HK$2.8276b pledges across 328 donors ([GitHub](https://github.com/immartian/Taipo-Big-Donations-Watcher.github.io)); major corporate/philanthropic pledges include Li Ka Shing Foundation, Lee Shau Kee Foundation, Chow Tai Fook, Sun Hung Kai Properties, Sino Group, Wharf, Hang Lung, HSBC/Hang Seng, BOCHK, AIA, Tencent, Alibaba, and others ([Ming Pao](https://finance.mingpao.com/fin/instantf/20251202/1764214289065/%E5%A4%A7%E5%9F%94%E5%AE%8F%E7%A6%8F%E8%8B%91%E4%BA%94%E7%B4%9A%E7%81%AB-%E6%9D%8E%E5%98%89%E8%AA%8D%E5%9F%BA%E9%87%91%E6%9C%83%E5%8C%AF%E8%B1%90%E4%B8%AD%E9%8A%80%E7%AD%89%E5%A4%9A%E9%96%93%E4%BC%81%E6%A5%AD%E6%8D%90%E6%AC%BE%E6%94%AF%E6%8F%B4%E3%80%90%E4%B8%8D%E6%96%B7%E6%9B%B4%E6%96%B0%E3%80%91#goog_rewarded)).
- **Resettlement (as of Mar 2026)**: On Feb 21, Deputy Financial Secretary Michael Wong announced a HK$6.8b buyback plan: HK$8,000/sq ft (without land premium) or HK$10,500/sq ft (with land premium); flat-for-flat exchanges available from 10 HOS/Housing Society projects. Redevelopment ruled out (9–10 year timeline). Budget (Feb 25) earmarks HK$4b from public funds + HK$2.8b from Support Fund. Survey showed 74% would consider acquisition, only 9% insist on redevelopment only. Some residents disappointed; hundreds signed a petition for redevelopment. Rehousing options: Shing Chi Court (Kowloon Bay, from Sep 2026), Chung Nga Road West/Kwong Fuk Park in Tai Po (2029, 2033), Kam Tin Wui Hei Court (late Aug 2027). Wang Chi House excluded from acquisition. Displaced residents remain in cramped temporary housing (~100–230 sq ft); 300+ residents petitioning for a face-to-face homeowners' meeting on renovation funds, fees, and building safety ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/society/article/3344228/wang-fuk-court-buyback-plan-disappoints-some-residents-despite-generous-offer) [Nikkei Asia](https://asia.nikkei.com/economy/fires/hong-kong-fire-survivors-hope-hearings-bring-yearned-for-answers)).
- **Human stories**: Survivor Yip Ka-kui described bamboo "exploding" and mesh igniting as foam-sealed windows trapped his wife Bai Shui Lin, who died after warning neighbors ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/hong-kong-apartment-fire-survivor-wife/)). The Collective HK profiled a Wang Tai House family: father, grandmother, Indonesian helper "Sandy," and 15-month-old "Yan Yan" died after a 7-hour call with relatives who say alarms stayed silent and foam-board-sealed windows left them stranded while firefighters said they could not "attack" 14/F ([The Collective HK](https://thecollectivehk.com/%E5%AE%8F%E7%A6%8F%E8%8B%91%E4%BA%94%E7%B4%9A%E7%81%AB%EF%BD%9C%E5%AE%B6%E5%B1%AC%E7%97%9B%E6%86%B6%E4%B8%83%E5%B0%8F%E6%99%82%E6%B1%82%E6%95%91%E9%80%9A%E8%A9%B1%E3%80%80%E5%A5%BD%E7%88%B8%E7%88%B8/?media_id=3786041531569256701_63468865757&media_author_id=63468865757&ranking_info_token=GCAwZDY5MDBlNjUzNTg0OTMyYTdjMTJlOTRlYjRhNDdiMiW4m6IFFdgEFoaG45MNGBMzNzg2MDQxNTMxNTY5MjU2NzAxKANuY2cA&utm_source=ig_text_feed_timeline)). Reuters profiled "Mr. Wong," the 71-year-old whose photo went viral while searching for his wife (Reuters/Tyrone Siu [story](https://www.reuters.com/investigates/special-report/hongkong-fire-wongfamily-picture/)). Filipino helper Maryan Pascual Esteban (40) died while protecting a child in her care; her son vowed to become a firefighter ([Threads](https://www.threads.com/?xmt=AQF0quUUcbnWbMwcu8qs7QiIGUwf-VbM0v3MC0n7vRG1og)). Another Filipino helper, Rhodora Alcaraz (28), who had arrived just one day before the fire, rescued her employer's 3-month-old baby and elderly mother; her panicked audio messages went viral and she was praised by Senator Imee Marcos as a "modern-day hero" ([AsiaOne](https://www.asiaone.com/asia/philippines-celebrates-heroic-helper-who-survived-hong-kong-fire)). The Collective HK profiled 5-year-old Cheng Hui-kiu and her helper Maryan: door cameras showed them leaving Wang Tai 24/F at 3:13 p.m. when corridors were smoke-free, but within 5 minutes dense smoke engulfed the floor; both died from smoke inhalation on lower floors, and parents call it a "man-made disaster" ([The Collective HK](https://thecollectivehk.com/%e5%ae%8f%e7%a6%8f%e8%8b%91%e7%81%ab%e7%81%bd%e4%ba%94%e6%ad%b2%e7%85%a6%e7%bf%b9%e8%8f%b2%e5%82%ad%e5%91%bd%e5%96%aa%e4%bd%8e%e5%b1%a4%e5%90%b8%e6%bf%83%e7%85%99%e8%87%b4%e6%ad%bb%e9%9b%99%e8%a6%aa/)). Nikkei Asia (Mar 18) profiled survivors ahead of hearings: Yip Ka-kui, 68, continued texting his wife daily after scattering her ashes at sea—"My wife must not die in vain"; Dorz Cheung, 33, lives in a ~110 sq ft ex-COVID isolation unit at Kai Tak, separated from his 87-year-old grandmother, suffering nightmares and nausea. HKSKH social worker Chan Mo-ning notes some families cannot visually ID remains and await DNA results ([am730](https://www.am730.com.hk/%E6%9C%AC%E5%9C%B0/%E5%A4%A7%E5%9F%94%E5%AE%8F%E7%A6%8F%E8%8B%91%E4%BA%94%E7%B4%9A%E7%81%AB-%E8%81%96%E5%85%AC%E6%9C%83-%E7%84%A1%E6%B3%95%E8%BE%A8%E8%AA%8D%E9%81%BA%E9%AB%94%E6%88%96%E9%9C%80%E6%95%B8%E6%9C%88%E9%A9%97dna-%E5%AE%B6%E5%B1%AC%E9%99%B7%E5%BE%AC%E5%BE%A8/624057)). As Lunar New Year 2026 approached, AP profiled displaced families: Phyllis Lo (48) made turnip cakes—a tradition inherited from her late mother—and said in tears "I really want to be with her"; 87-year-old Pearl Chow joked she might "go to her heavenly home" before rebuilding is complete; 23-year-old Isaac Tam worries his 92-year-old grandfather cannot wait for resettlement ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/hong-kong-resettlement-fire-tai-po-2caedfdaee6f9460351e257cdcaeef1d)).
