## Legal Case and Convictions

In July 2021, five members of the speech therapists' union were arrested and charged with "conspiracy to publish seditious publications" under a colonial-era sedition law:

- **Lorie Lai** (é»é›¯é½¡) - Union chairperson
- **Melody Yeung** (æ¥Šé€¸æ„) - External vice-chairperson
- **Sidney Ng** (å³æ¢“ç‘‹)
- **Samuel Chan** (é™³æºæ£®)
- **Marco Fong** (æ–¹æ¢“çš“)

All five, aged between 25 and 28 at the time of arrest, were denied bail and held in pre-trial detention for over 13 months.

**September 2022 Conviction:** In September 2022, all five defendants were found guilty and sentenced to 19 months in prison. The judge ruled the books were an "exercise in brainwashing" meant to incite hatred toward the government. The five were released shortly after sentencing, with credit for time served during pre-trial detention.

**Defiant Statements:** During sentencing, some maintained their stance:
- Melody Yeung told the court she had "no regrets" and only wished she had "published more picture books" before her arrest
- Lorie Lai attempted to argue that her only "crime" was trying to tell a story from the people's perspective

**Aftermath:**
- The General Union of Hong Kong Speech Therapists was de-registered and disbanded by the government
- In late 2024, the High Court ordered the seizure of the defunct union's remaining assets (approximately HK$116,000) on national security grounds
- Since 2023, individuals in Hong Kong have been arrested and sentenced to prison merely for importing or possessing copies of these books, which are now classified as "seditious publications"

## The Three Books 

The first book, titled "Guardians of Sheep Village" (ç¾Šæ‘å®ˆè¡›è€…), explains the 2019 pro-democracy protests that swept through Hong Kong.

"Janitors of Sheep Village" (ç¾Šæ‘æ¸…é“å¤«), the second book, sees cleaners in the village go on strike to force out wolves who leave litter everywhere. The book's introduction explains it is a reference to Hong Kong medical workers striking in February 2020 in a bid to force the government to close the border with mainland China at the start of the coronavirus pandemic.

The final book in the trilogy -- "The 12 Braves of Sheep Village" (ç¾Šæ‘åäºŒå‹‡å£«) -- is about a group of sheep who flee their village by boat because of the wolves. It references the "Hong Kong 12," a group of activists captured by the Chinese coastguard while attempting to flee to Taiwan.
